This hazardous weather outlook is for portions of Central Kansas,

.DAY ONE...Tonight

No hazardous weather expected at this time.  

.DAYS TWO THROUGH SEVEN...Saturday through Thursday

Very high grassland fire danger will exist Saturday areawide,
highest generally along and southeast of the Kansas Turnpike. 

Very strong and gusty south winds of 25 to 35 mph are expected
Sunday, with gusts likely exceeding 50 to 55 mph for many. This 
will lead to elevated fire danger across the entire area Sunday, 
most critical from central Kansas on westward. Also, there is a low
chance for an isolated strong to marginally severe thunderstorm 
Sunday evening generally west of the Kansas Turnpike. Stay tuned for
later forecasts.

The potential for widespread meaningful rainfall is increasing
Monday through early Tuesday across the region, especially over 
southeast Kansas. Widespread amounts of 1 to 2 inches are possible, 
with locally higher amounts. Please stay tuned for later forecasts.
