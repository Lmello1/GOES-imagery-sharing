...FREEZE WARNING IN EFFECT FROM MIDNIGHT MONDAY NIGHT THROUGH 
TUESDAY MORNING...

* WHAT...Sub-freezing temperatures ranging from 28 to 32, with
  isolated locations as low as 26 possible.

* WHERE...Colder valley locations east and south of Tucson,
  including portions of Santa Cruz, Graham, Greenlee, and Cochise
  counties. Specifically, portions of Upper Santa Cruz River
  Valley/Altar Valley, Upper San Pedro River Valley, Eastern
  Cochise County below 5000 feet including the Sulphur Springs
  Valley, and Upper Gila River Valley.

* WHEN...From midnight Monday night through Tuesday morning.

* IMPACTS...Frost and freeze conditions will kill crops, other
  sensitive vegetation and possibly damage unprotected outdoor
  plumbing.

PRECAUTIONARY/PREPAREDNESS ACTIONS...

Take steps now to protect tender plants from the cold. To prevent
freezing and possible bursting of outdoor water pipes they should
be wrapped, drained, or allowed to drip slowly. Those that have
in-ground sprinkler systems should drain them and cover above-
ground pipes to protect them from freezing.
